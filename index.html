Here's an overview of our project.

This project aims to look over the effects of spreading fake news, quantify the spread of fake news on twitter users and to investigate the effects of satire tweets on the public and its relation to fake news.

Motivation

With social media being part of people’s daily life. It is very likely that they would encounter fake news from different social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, Youtube, and many more. For this project, the group will be investigating fake news on Twitter.

Problem

According to Babcock, Beskow, and Carley (2018), online satire posts are also considered fake news. Satire responses were found to be shared more than non-satire tweets. It was also found that satire responses were able to fool some users in believing that the tweet or posts were real. With this, we plan to investigate the views and effects of the topic, Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda', on the twitter audience in the Philippines.

Solution

The group’s goal is to make people, specifically the twitter users and audiences, more aware of satirical posts and its effects on other users as some might end up believing it and sharing it more.

Here is our Problem Formulation:

Research Question

Does being satirical affect the number of engagements on the mis/disinformation tweets about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda'?

Hypothesis

Satire tweets about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda' have more engagement than non-satire tweets.

Null Hypothesis

There is no significant difference on the engagements of Satire and Non-Satire posts about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda'.

Action Plan

Analyze the frequency of mis/disinformation about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda'.

Data Collection Criteria

The data or tweets collected must be verified as mis/disinformation. They should also be fact-checked by a trusted fact-checking organization. These tweets must be posted from 2016 to 2022 and should be unique and not retweets.

Preprocessing

Handling missing values/ensuring no missing values

To handle the missing values, we will be imputing them. In doing so, it will make sure that there are no blank values. For example, since the location of the user in twitter is optional, those who do not have any location placed will be changed to “No Location” instead of a blank. The optional columns (quote tweets, views, rating etc) will also be removed since not all have those information.

Handling outliers

The only outliers we expect are tweets with high amounts of engagement (i.e. likes, retweets, replies). However, such tweets are also the most likely to provide significant insights regarding engagement patterns, so we have opted to keep them as is.

Ensuring formatting consistency (date, labels, etc.)

To ensure that the format is consistent, we will be checking the dates and make sure that the date joined is in the correct format. Having a consistent format will help in the analzation of data as it ensures that the data retrieved is correct since it has the same cnsistent formatting.

Normalization/ standardization/ scaling

The two sets of variables that will primarily be used are engagement and satirical content. In the case that multivariate analysis will be performed due to more than one engagement variable being used, language being considered (see next point below), or something similar, we will standardize the data. This is because normalization is more sensitive to outliers, which we have decided to keep.

Categorical data encoding

The main focus of this project lies in satirical vs. non-satirical tweets, and we will simply assign the value 0 to a tweet identified as satire and 1 otherwise. However, we are also looking into separating tweets by language; for example, English, Tagalog, Taglish, or others.

Statistical Hypothesis Test

Perform the appropriate statistical test based on the hypothesis.

For this topic, we will be using the chi-square goodness of fit test in testing our hypothesis - Satire tweets about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda' have more engagement than non-satire tweets.

Identify and validate the assumptions of the chosen statistical model. Discuss any violations of assumptions and their potential impact on the results.

To test the significant association of engagements (likes, retweets and replies) and satire content on the tweets, a chi-square statistical test was used. The p-value measures the evidence against the null hypothesis. With this, setting the alpha value to 5% may impact the results as there is a smaller margin.

From the data, it can be seen that 47 of the tweets are satire (31%) and 103 of the tweets are non-satire (69%). With this, assuming that the expected value to 40% of the total number of engagements for satire and 60% of the total number of engagements of non-satire may also impact the results.

Clearly report the test statistics, p-values, and any effect size measures.

Individually, the likes and retweets’ p-value is greater than the confidence level and their chi-square value is less than the critical value. Allowing us to accept the null hypothesis. However, for the replies, the p-value is less than the confidence level and their chi-square value is greater than the critical value. Making us reject the null hypothesis. This may be because likes and retweets can only be done once but replies can be made multiple times.

Looking at the combined engagements and based on the hypothesis test, the p-value is greater than 0.05 (95%) confidence level. Additionally, the chi-square value is less than the critical value. With this, we accept the null hypothesis “There is no significant difference on the engagements of Satire and Non-Satire posts about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda'.”.

Introduction

Social Media is integrated into the lives of almost everyone, making it likely for users to encounter mis/disinformation across different social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, TikTok and etc. There are different forms of mis/disinformation and satirical posts are considered to be one of them since these posts are able to fool users into believing it is real.

During the COVID 19 Pandemic in the Philippines, “ayuda” were given by the LGUs to people to support them during lockdown. Trying to help the Filipino people, Sen. Alan Cayetano advocated to give a 10,000 peso “ayuda” to the Filipino families. However, there were people who did not know it was just a proposal and believed that it will actually happen while some joked around and made satirical posts about this.

With this, the group will investigate the views and effects of the topic, Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k “ayuda” on the twitter audience in the Philippines with the goal of making twitter users and audience more aware of satirical posts and its effects in other users as some might end up believing it and sharing it more.

Materials and Methods

Step 1. Topic Formulation

Looking for a topic was the most important part in our research. The group decided on the topic “Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k Ayuda”. The goal of the group is to analyze satire and non-satire tweets.

Step 2. Problem Formulation

After deciding on a topic, the group formulated the research question, hypothesis, null hypothesis and action plan.

Step 3. Data Collection

With this, the group gathered data from Twitter. These data are tweets from 2016 to 2022. Snscrape and the keywords “cayetano 10k ayuda” were used to filter out and to look for the exact tweets needed. These tweets are then placed in an excel file and categorized into either satire or non-satire tweets.

Step 4. Preprocessing

From the collected data, missing values and formatting were checked to make sure that everything is consistent and there are no empty boxes. In classifying the tweets into satire and non-satire. Satire is categorized as 0 and non-satire is categorized as 1.

Step 5. Data Visualization

Using the complete data, graphs and charts were made to further understand the trends and relationships of the data

Step 6. Statistical Test and Machine Learning

Using the Chi-Square Test (statistical) and the satire classifier, the group was able to understand the data more.

Step 7. Machine Learning Model

The group made a satire analyzer, which has a 66% accuracy in predicting if the text is satire or non-satire.

Results and Discussion

From the statistical test, we found out that the null hypothesis is correct. There is no significant difference on the engagements of Satire and Non-Satire posts about Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda'. Since satire is considered as a form of fake news, it is important to take note of this and not be swayed

Implications

Knowing that satirical contents in posts can still gather engagements from people. It is important to take note that even joking around can give out the wrong idea or image as some people won’t know whether the post is an actual fact or a joke.

Tweets containing satirical words or comments does not affect the number of engagements it will receive. However, satirical posts are considered to be misinformation posts. Thus, making it important for people to know not to do this so that they will not confuse other users./p>
