Here's an overview of our project.

This project aims to look over the effects of spreading fake news, quantify the spread of fake news on twitter users and to investigate the effects of satire tweets on the public and its relation to fake news.

Motivation

With social media being part of people’s daily life. It is very likely that they would encounter fake news from different social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, Youtube, and many more. For this project, the group will be investigating fake news on Twitter.

Problem

According to Babcock, Beskow, and Carley (2018), online satire posts are also considered fake news. Satire responses were found to be shared more than non-satire tweets. It was also found that satire responses were able to fool some users in believing that the tweet or posts were real. With this, we plan to investigate the views and effects of the topic, Sen. Alan Cayetano 10k 'ayuda', on the twitter audience in the Philippines.

Solution

The group’s goal is to make people, specifically the twitter users and audiences, more aware of satirical posts and its effects on other users as some might end up believing it and sharing it more.
